Executive Summary

The Problem, the Solution, the Impact

BorrowBox is a city-wide shared equipment lending service where residents reserve camping gear, tools, electronics, and household items from five hub locations. Equipment must be returned by a scheduled due date — but 23% of all 2,100 loans were returned late.

Of those late returns, 63.7% caused a reservation disruption — the next borrower arrived at a hub and found their item still checked out. The existing reminder system was tested and found statistically ineffective: 22.7% late rate with reminder vs. 23.4% without — a 0.7% difference.

Staff only discovered problems after due dates passed, forcing entirely reactive operations that could not prevent downstream failures.

Solution: We built two integrated components — a 6-tab storytelling analytics dashboard and a 6-screen operational prototype — that give staff and administrators visibility into where, what, and why late returns happen, and a proactive At-Risk Return Monitor that flags items before the due date passes.
Result: Late Return Rate fell from 23.0% to 11.4% (-50.4%). Disruption Rate from 63.7% to 28.1% (-55.9%). Average delay from 51.5h to 18.2h (-64.7%). At-Risk Detection Rate rose from 0% to 64.9%. Staff Override Rate fell from 8.9% to 2.9% (-67.4%).
Key finding: Camping equipment (31.2% LRR) and HUB5 (28.4% LRR) drive the majority of late returns. These two dimensions now receive the highest weights in the risk scoring formula.

HUB5 is the Outlier

HUB5 has an LRR of 28.4% — 5.4 points above the 23.0% system average. It also leads on disruptions (110 events) and ARD (62.3h). The same policy produces vastly different outcomes by location.

Camping equipment has the highest LRR (31.2%) and longest ARD (68.4h). This corrected an early assumption that Tools would rank highest. Camping gear is taken on multi-day trips where exact return timing is hard to control.

Section 08 — Ethical & Social Considerations

Responsibility in Data-Driven Operations

🔒

Data Privacy

The system processes borrower contact details and transaction histories. A production deployment requires encryption at rest and in transit, role-based access control, and a clear data retention policy. This academic implementation uses fully synthetic data — no real personal information.

⚖️

Algorithmic Bias

The risk score assigns higher weights to HUB5 and Camping equipment based on historical patterns. If those patterns reflect socioeconomic factors — e.g., a hub in a neighbourhood with transportation barriers — the system may disproportionately flag borrowers from that location. Production deployments must audit outcomes across demographic groups.

♿

Inclusion & Accessibility

The dashboard and prototype are web-based, requiring a modern browser. The self-contained HTML files work offline, benefiting staff in low-connectivity locations. Accessibility features (screen reader compatibility, sufficient colour contrast) would be required additions before any live deployment.

Section 09 — Lessons Learned & Team Reflection

What We Built, What We Learned, What's Next

Most Important Analytical Lesson

Our initial assumption that Tools would be the highest-risk equipment category was wrong. The data clearly showed Camping at 31.2% versus Tools at 23.1%. This required revising the DPAD template, dashboard chart labels, risk scoring weights, and all documentation. The experience reinforced: assumptions must always be tested against data before being embedded in a solution design.

The Value of Connecting Insight to Action

The first version delivered only the dashboard. Adding the operational prototype — particularly the At-Risk Monitor — transformed the project from a reporting tool into a decision support system. That distinction is what produced the 67.4% SOR reduction: staff didn't just see the problem differently, they had a different workflow for acting on it (McKinsey, 2020).

Professor Feedback Changes Made

Five concrete changes: (1) APA citations added to all 10 dataset generation rules. (2) Axis labels added to all 18 dashboard charts. (3) Plain-language insight callouts on every chart. (4) Dashboard restructured from a flat chart collection into a 6-tab narrative. (5) Data figures corrected and Camping correctly identified as highest-risk category.
